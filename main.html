Because of this I decided I'd run Jason Stathams big vacation which I thought everyone would enjoy and at the beginning when the CRs group only had the one person we planned to combine groups and I'll do it for both groups play CR. However just as they came to sit down the other two from their group arrived and they went off to do theirs. Jason Stathams big vacation is still runnable with 3 players so I tried to carry on. So halfway through the person who was blind to it all got up, took their character sheet and sat down at CRs table. At that moment the dude on his phone decided to tell everyone that this was his last session. I kinda got bored after that point as I couldn't run anything and instead worked on the upcoming boss fight. This session we had a new member join us, they had planned to come earlier but we're busy I whipped out the character sheet that I made for them and brought them into the campaign. At the moment they play a high elf druid but that's subject to change. For the first half we still had the guy who said he'd left but he did leave. Our new member was also accompanied by a friend of ours who offered to teach them how to play as we go on. So this is what happened, as the party stood on the deck of the ship with bodies left right and centre a noise came from inside of the mast. A faint clapping sound and as it got louder a physical form seemed to come from the mast, a tall figure with flowing hair. Around her neck was a key, she did not remember owning a key but assumed it came from the mast. As she emerged she looked around and at the end of the bow of the ship she could see a similar key. She told everyone about this who had previously tried to break into the captains quarters, it seemed to be protected by magic. One of the other party members went out to get it and due to the previous stormy days it was hard and she almost fell off. On her way back down she was able to slide down the bow and onto deck. She tried to use the key to unlock the lock on the door but it didn't seem to work fully, the party decided to try the other key to only get the same effect. This is when the only male in our group decided to look for a second lock, before realising it was big enough for two keys and yanked it from their owners and unlocked and opened the door. As he opened the door he saw familiar boots, then trousers, then torso he knew what he was looking at and how it was useless to look up top as the Captains head was chopped of a day ago but he did anyway. Only to find a purple firey wisp with 2 yellow streaks for eyes. As soon as the door had opened it started to speak in a snarling rough voice "what was it this body said, look after her for me? She could of told us everything and saved herself but she chose her boat instead." At that point it grabbed the sword from the desk and imitated combat from behind the party an arrow cane flying hitting the creature then a sword then some magic that made the room half as big due to the ice taking up the other half. The wispy creature turned to the man who opened the door said some Latin and burned dimmer. In the eyes of the man who opened the door there was now a purple fire and he took out his sword and attempted to attack another but by the sheer terrible accuracy of the host he missed. At that point another arrow flew through the crowd hitting the creatures side but not landing, then a ray of frost was sent towards the creature who took out his sword and sliced back at that point a final arrow flew making it fall flat and the purple wispy flame thing disappeared. On the desk of the captain was a scroll with a red seal. On it was written the party's names including there new member. On the other side it said, you will know how to use it, and tell your new friend sneak into boats at night to not get caught. On the inside was a map with glowing red crosses. 20/12/2018

If you are reading this then that means you have some understanding of how to play dungeons and dragons, or at least what it is. I myself have been playing for little under a year now and have DM'd for two groups. Early into the school year a Friend of mine and the guy who got me into D&D said to me he wanted to run a club at lunchtimes for D&D. As I was already running a club I offered to help out, and so he got to work running clubs was somewhat uncommon for people of our year but we were both trusted individuals and granted the permission. I was never too sure what he wanted to do with the club on the beginning so I suggested some of my own ideas. After a few weeks of work we had created a presentation to last us a bit about playing D&D or so we thought, we had to cut the first 2 weeks of it short due to us not having enough stuff. We had eventually got enough for a full lunchtime, this being 55 minutes, and so we ran that power point and since then we’ve been creating characters with everyone. Last week we were finishing off peoples characters and seeing who wanted to do what campaign, we had 3 campaigns, mine which is a series of one shots that are usually written the night before, the club runners who has adapted an official campaign (a heist one) then our other friend and current DM who is running one based of his homebrew campaign if I remember correctly. Everyone in my groups characters were finished when last weeks club reached half way so I went up to the club runner and said “hey, my groups all done by the looks of it. Is it alright if I start the campaign?” which is when he turned to me and told me we never actually told them how to play D&D, so I raced over to my laptop and loaded up the PowerPoint and started adding slides left right and centre so that we could start soon. At the time of me writing this post I am what I think is half way through how to actually play. I have a feeling I will have left some stuff out but if it’s important they can ask their DM, if they ask me they’ll probably get this response “ I don’t know ask one of the dudes walking around answering questions.”
